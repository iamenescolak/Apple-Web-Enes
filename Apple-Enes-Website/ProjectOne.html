Project Overview

This case study focuses on the design and development process of Notefege, a mobile app designed to assist users in reading sheet music and understanding solfege. The goal was to create a user-friendly app with high fidelity designs within a two-week deadline. This article provides an overview of the typography, colors, research, personas, wireframes, low fidelity and high fidelity designs, as well as usability studies conducted. All of this was done with Figma.

The goal of creating Notefege was to design and develop a user-friendly mobile app that helps individuals, regardless of their level of music theory knowledge, in reading sheet music and understanding solfege. The app aims to provide a seamless and intuitive experience for users, allowing them to easily access and interpret sheet music, play the pitch of notes, set the desired Beats Per Minute (BPM), and enhance their music theory understanding. The ultimate objective is to ensure that users, whether beginners or experienced musicians, can confidently read sheet music and enjoy a smoother music learning and playing experience.

Problem Statement

Aspiring musicians and individuals learning to read sheet music face challenges in understanding and interpreting musical notations, particularly solfege.

Expected Outcomes & Benefits

Notefege is expected to deliver several significant outcomes and benefits for its users. Firstly, the app will enhance users' music reading skills by providing clear and intuitive visual representations of sheet music and solfege. This will enable users to accurately interpret and play musical notations. Secondly, Notefege will improve users' understanding of music theory through features such as playing the pitch of notes and providing educational resources. This will empower users to deepen their knowledge and become more proficient musicians. Additionally, the app will boost users' confidence in their music performances by offering a user-friendly interface and interactive features that facilitate practice and accuracy. Notefege will also provide seamless access to sheet music, allowing users to easily import PDFs from platforms like Google Drive or Dropbox. Furthermore, the app will offer a personalized learning experience by accommodating different clefs, keys, and instruments. By optimizing practice sessions and providing immediate feedback, Notefege will save users time and enhance their efficiency in music learning. Lastly, the app will make music education more accessible by catering to users with varying levels of music theory knowledge, fostering a love for music and enabling lifelong engagement. Overall, Notefege aims to revolutionize the way individuals learn and engage with sheet music, providing an enriching and enjoyable musical experience for all users.

Planning Phase

To ensure a user-centric approach in designing Notefege, I conducted thorough research to understand the behaviors, needs, and pain points of potential users. Through creating an empathy map and developing a primary persona named "Users," I gained valuable insights into their preferences and motivations. This deep understanding guided the design process, resulting in an app that caters to the specific requirements of Users. By prioritizing user needs, Notefege delivers a seamless and intuitive experience, meeting the expectations of diverse individuals who will benefit from the app's features and functionalities.

Stakeholders

The primary stakeholders of Notefege are aspiring musicians and individuals learning to read sheet music. These users will be the primary customers and beneficiaries of the Notefege mobile app. The app aims to cater to individuals of varying levels of music theory knowledge, providing support and guidance to beginners as well as advanced musicians. The goal is to assist users in reading sheet music and understanding solfege, ultimately enhancing their music reading skills and overall musical performance.

Primary Persona

Ali

Ali is a passionate musician who loves playing the piano, guitar, and singing. He has a basic understanding of reading sheet music but struggles with music theory. Ali has tried using various websites and apps, but they often make it harder for him to learn. As a busy professional, he wants a user-friendly app that simplifies the process of reading sheet music and helps him understand music theory. Ali is motivated to improve his music skills and seeks an app that provides clear explanations, examples, and interactive features to support his learning.

Empathy Mapping

ALI- What do they Think and Feel?

Ali feels overwhelmed when trying to read sheet music and understand music theory. He thinks that learning music theory is challenging and time-consuming. Ali is frustrated with other websites that make it harder for him to read sheet music. However, he feels motivated and excited to improve his music skills.

How do they Feel?

Ali feels a sense of accomplishment and satisfaction when he successfully reads and interprets sheet music. He is curious and eager to explore different musical pieces and genres. Ali may feel a bit overwhelmed when encountering complex sheet music or unfamiliar musical terms. However, he feels relieved and supported when he receives guidance and assistance in understanding music theory.

What do they say?

He mentions the need for clear explanations and examples to understand musical concepts. Ali may express frustration with apps or websites that have confusing interfaces or lack useful features. What do they do?

Ali actively searches for resources and tools to enhance his music reading abilities. He practices playing musical instruments and attempts to read sheet music on his own. Ali seeks assistance from music teachers or fellow musicians to understand music theory and improve his skills. Ali seeks an app that simplifies the process of reading sheet music and provides clear explanations and examples to enhance his understanding.
